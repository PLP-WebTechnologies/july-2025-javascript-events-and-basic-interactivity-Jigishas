Coffee Plant Care FAQ

Your guide to growing healthy coffee plants at home

How much light does a coffee plant need?

Coffee plants thrive in bright, indirect light. They should receive at least 4-6 hours of sunlight daily, but avoid direct afternoon sun which can scorch their leaves. East or west-facing windows are ideal. If natural light is insufficient, consider using a grow light.

How often should I water my coffee plant?

Water your coffee plant when the top inch of soil feels dry. This is typically once a week, but can vary based on temperature and humidity. Ensure proper drainage to prevent root rot. Reduce watering frequency in winter when the plant's growth slows down.

What is the ideal temperature for coffee plants?

Coffee plants prefer temperatures between 65°F and 80°F (18°C - 27°C). They are sensitive to cold and should be kept away from drafts and sudden temperature changes. Avoid temperatures below 55°F (13°C) as this can damage the plant.

How do I fertilize my coffee plant?

Use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer every 4-6 weeks during the growing season (spring and summer). Reduce fertilization to every 8-10 weeks in fall and winter. Coffee plants prefer slightly acidic soil, so occasionally using an acid-forming fertilizer can be beneficial.

Why are the leaves of my coffee plant turning brown?

Brown leaves can indicate several issues: underwatering, low humidity, too much direct sunlight, or fertilizer burn. Check your watering schedule, ensure the plant isn't in direct sun, and consider misting the leaves or using a humidifier to increase humidity around the plant.

When will my coffee plant produce beans?

Coffee plants typically begin to produce flowers and berries after 3-5 years of growth. The berries contain the coffee beans. Plants grown indoors may take longer to fruit and may require hand pollination by gently shaking the flowers or using a small brush to transfer pollen.

How do I prune my coffee plant?

Prune your coffee plant in early spring before new growth begins. Remove any dead or damaged branches, and trim back leggy growth to maintain a bushy shape. You can also pinch off the top of the plant to encourage branching. Always use clean, sharp pruning shears.
